Privacy Policy

The Everyday Kitchen · Last updated

This page explains what data The Everyday Kitchen collects, where it goes, and how to remove it. Short version: almost everything stays on your device.

What we store locally on your device

Your preferences, the recipes you've liked or disliked, your shopping day, and the ingredients you've marked as "already at home" are saved in your browser's localStorage. This data never leaves your device unless you explicitly trigger AI generation (see below).

What we send to our servers

The site itself does not have a user database. The only time information leaves your device is when you tap the "Suggest something new" button. That action sends:

  • Your taste preferences (macro balance, flavor mood, who's at the table)
  • Names of recipes you've liked and disliked

This payload is forwarded to Anthropic's API to generate a new recipe matching your tastes. We do not log or persist this data on our servers. Anthropic's data handling is described in their privacy policy.

What we do not collect

  • Your name, email, phone, or any contact information
  • Your IP address (beyond what's needed to prevent abuse of the AI feature)
  • Location data
  • Browser fingerprints, advertising identifiers, or analytics cookies

Cookies and tracking

We do not use cookies. We do not use Google Analytics, Facebook Pixel, or any third-party tracker.

How to delete your data

Tap "Reset" in the app's header, or clear your browser's site data for this domain. Both actions remove everything we've stored.

Children

This app is not directed at children under 13 and we do not knowingly collect data from children.

Changes

If we ever change what data is collected or where it goes, we'll update this page and bump the "last updated" date.
